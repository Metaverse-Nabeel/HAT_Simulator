# Sample Questions & Syllabus Patterns

Use these patterns to model new questions for the HAT Simulator.

## 1. Verbal Reasoning
Tests vocabulary, reading comprehension, and sentence structure.
*   **Vocabulary/Synonyms**: e.g. Choose the word that is similar in meaning to "DEBACLE". (A: Fiasco)
*   **Sentence Completion**: e.g. "It’s time she \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ some work." (A: did)
*   **Analogies**: e.g. BEE: HONEY:: (A: cow: milk)
*   **Reading Comprehension**: A long paragraph about history, science, or social studies, followed by 3-5 questions deducing facts, implied meanings, or identifying the main idea.

## 2. Quantitative Reasoning
Tests mathematical aptitude at the 16-year education level (Algebra, Geometry, Arithmetic).
*   **Equations**: If 8^n = 16^4 then n = ?
*   **Word Problems**: The sum of a number and its reciprocal is equal to four times the difference... Find the number.
*   **Ratios**: 5a + 3b / 2a - 3b = 23/5, find a : b.
*   **Averages/Stats**: The average of 5 numbers is 12.6. If four numbers are 9, 12, 15, and 20, what is the 5th number?

## 3. Analytical Reasoning
Tests logical deduction using condition-based rules (Games, Arrangements, Selection).
*   **Scenario Format**: 
    "In a large factory there are exactly seven supervisors... F, G, H, J, K, M, N. Messages can be sent in only one direction from F to G, from G to J... Messages can be sent in either direction between G and H..."
*   **Question Format**:
    "Sending a message from the first to the second supervisor in which of the following pairs requires exactly three intermediaries?"
    "If office 5 is painted white, which of the following could be true?"
*   *Note*: Ensure that analytical questions follow strict logical bounds without multiple interpretations.

## 4. Difficulty Tagging Guidelines
- `Easy`: Direct application of a formula, straightforward synonym, or basic one-step logic.
- `Medium`: Requires 2-3 logical steps, combining two math concepts, or analogy with tricky distractors.
- `Hard`: Complex multi-step math word problems, dense reading comprehension paragraphs, heavily constrained analytical games.
- `Super Hard`: Edge-case mathematics, obscure vocabulary, or analytical games requiring extensive mapping of variables.
